RWIND Practice Simulations

This section documents my practice simulations conducted using RWIND. The simulations examine wind–structure interaction around large urban geometries under different inflow conditions. And PALM(LES) for urban-induced turbulence and OpenFOAM(LES) for vehicle-induced vortices will be added soon...

The analysis includes surface pressure, pressure coefficients (Cp), velocity fields, and streamlines, with emphasis on distinguishing surface quantities from field quantities and understanding their relevance for Advanced Air Mobility (AAM) safety assessments.

Screenshots and short videos are provided below to demonstrate a methodological understanding of CFD using RWIND3.
