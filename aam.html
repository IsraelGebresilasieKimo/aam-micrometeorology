Advanced Air Mobility & Urban Wind

Advanced Air Mobility operations occur within the atmospheric boundary layer, where wind shear, turbulence, and building-induced wakes strongly affect flight safety and efficiency. Urban environments introduce complex microclimatic effects that must be assessed using high-resolution modeling approaches.

I use CFD tools such as RWIND to investigate pressure fields, velocity distributions, turbulence characteristics, and wake structures around urban geometries. My focus is on interpreting these results in terms of operational risk for low-altitude aerial vehicles.
